Indeginous Reserves Living Conditions.

The positive right to adequate shelter is a right that all humans should be entitled to, and one that Canada claims to afford it’s citizens, however this is far from the case with Indeginous peoples.

Life outside the Reserves

There are many dire living conditions that many Indeginous people face inside the reserves, so the question can then be asked, why do they simply not leave the reserves? The housing crisis for Indigenous people is just as bad outside the reserves. Due to discriminatory practices, along with the overall Canadian housing market, finding housing is nearly impossible.

What can we do to make a difference?

Seeing the dire conditions that thousands of indeginous people have to go through everyday, it is evident that there needs to be widespread change, as this is a violation of not only their constitutional rights, but their fundamental human rights as well.
